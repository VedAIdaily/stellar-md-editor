Support

Stellar MD Editor is a free, refreshingly simple editor for plain-text and Markdown files stored in Google Drive™, published by VedAIspace (vedaispace.com). This page answers common questions and tells you how to reach us.

Contact

For help, questions, or feedback, email info (at) vedaispace (dot) com. We read everything and usually reply within a few days.

How do I open a file?

In Google Drive, right-click a .txt or .md file, choose "Open with", then "Stellar MD Editor". While editing, the Open button next to Save lets you switch to another file without leaving the editor. The app can only access files you open with it or create through it.

Can I open files with a double-click, without the right-click menu?

Yes, with a one-time Drive setting: in Google Drive, click the gear icon, then "Settings", then "Manage apps", find Stellar MD Editor, and tick "Use by default". From then on, double-clicking a .txt or .md file opens it in the editor directly.

Does it work on my phone?

Yes. Open md.vedaispace.com in your phone's browser, tap "Open a file from Drive", and pick the file; editing and saving work the same as on desktop. The Google Drive mobile app cannot launch web editors from its "Open with" menu (a platform limitation that applies to all web apps), so start from this page instead.

Where do my changes go?

Click Save or press Ctrl+S (Cmd+S on Mac). The status bar shows "Saved to Drive", and your changes are written back to the same file in your Google Drive. The app stores nothing anywhere else; open the file again at any time to continue where you left off.

I typed **bold** but the text does not look bold

That is by design. Stellar MD Editor is a plain-text editor: it shows and saves the Markdown source exactly as written, with no formatted preview. The formatting appears wherever the file is viewed or published (websites, wikis, and apps that render Markdown).

How do I create a new file?

In Google Drive, click "New", then "More", then "Stellar MD Editor"; an Untitled.md file is created in the current folder. You can also use the "New file in My Drive" button on the app's home page.

How do I rename a file?

Click the file name at the top of the editor, type the new name, and press Enter.

Which permissions does the app use?

Per-file access only (the drive.file scope): the app can see just the files you open with it or create through it, never the rest of your Drive. Details are in the Privacy Policy.
